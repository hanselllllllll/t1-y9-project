A Good Girl's Guide to Murder
Rene Magritte made this painting because he was commissioned by his friend, Harry Torczyner. Harry commissioned Rene to make a self-portrait, and so he did. Except his face is covered by a floating green apple. He covered his face because of the anonymity and how he has to keep his identity a secret in business. “Everything we see hides another thing, we always want to see what is hidden by what we see,” Magritte explained.
